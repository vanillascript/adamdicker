    Emails do stand up in court but only if they meet the material needs of the contract. I doubt your emails had specific terms related to your options in case of warranty, liability, failure to deliver, etc. etc. In other words - technically your email may be a contract but it's not a contract. A small business working professionally will be very clear - mostly to protect themselves from dissatisfied customers (for example someone who spends $3000 and expects the worlds greatest custom website end "free" maintenance or over-the-top-treat-me-like-Im-family extended warranties)

    Small claims court (which wouldn't work in this case) is typically for damages under $5000 and depending on jurisdiction a lawyer may not even be allowed - but certainly not cost effective when looking for < $5000.

    Basically - domainers need to stop short-cutting if they want quality.
